The Topic

In the era of widely avaliable fast internet, online video content consumption is how most people get their entertainmaent. Advertisers have to result to pop-ups, pre-rolls, and interuptive ads to broad cast their message. They rely on the content creators to capture people’s attention. And right when the viewer is captivated …. They are interrupted with an ad. But this breaks the engagement and leaves viewers irritated. Which sets up an interesting relationship between the brands who need to advertise their products, content creators who want to monitize and viewers who just want to be intertained.

The Problem

Pop-ups, page takeovers, online ads that play automatically and commercials that interrupt someone’s streaming experience: these are just some of the ways brands try to get consumers to notice them. One of the most popular forms of online ad format is the “pre-roll,” which plays before a video, and can be skipped after a certain amount of time.
IPG’s figures suggest that about 65 percent of people skip online video advertising, and they do so as soon as they get the chance. Most people skip ads out of habit, with 76 percent saying they do so because it’s an ingrained behavior.
Additionally, Pre-roll ads and interupting ads are exponetially more irritating in the eyes of viewers during live streams because the viewer is forced to miss live stream content. 

The Market

“Live video grew by 93%, with an average viewing time of 26.4 minutes per session.” in 2019. (1)  Live streamers can build a sense of connection with their viewers unlike recorded content.  Videos hold users’ attention 10-20x longer than pre-recorded, or on-demand content. (2) Of all people who report that they watch live streams, 7 out of 10 of them tune in to a live stream daily. (3) Millennials are the largest group of consumers of live content. 63% of people ages 18-34 are watching live-streaming content regularly. (4)
